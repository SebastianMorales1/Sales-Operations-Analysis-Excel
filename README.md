# Portfolio Project: Sales & Commissions Analysis with Excel

## Project Overview
This project is a **professional simulation** designed to demonstrate advanced Excel capabilities in a business environment, specifically focusing on logistics and sales operations. The objective was to transform a raw dataset of 450+ records into an actionable tool for calculating sales commissions and analyzing regional performance.

📌 Project Overview
This project provides a comprehensive analysis of sales performance across US regions and sales representatives using an interactive Excel dashboard. By transforming raw transaction data into visual insights, this tool enables stakeholders to monitor key dynamic graphics, identify operational gaps, and drive data-driven growth strategies.

### 1. Regional Dominance (Donut Chart)
- The Sales Distribution by Region shows a heavy concentration of revenue in two specific areas.
- The Insight: The West (8,873)∗∗and∗∗North(8,709) regions are our primary powerhouses, together accounting for over 65% of total sales
- Strategic Recommendation: There is a significant performance gap in the South and East regions. We should focus on replicating the logistics and sales tactics of the West/North regions in these underperforming areas to unlock hidden market potential

### 2. Team Efficiency & Dependency (Horizontal Bar Chart)
- The Sales Performance by Salesperson chart reveals a high reliance on top-tier talent.
- The "Pete" Benchmark: Pete is the clear leader with $7,834 in sales—nearly double the average of his peers
- He serves as our efficiency benchmark, maintaining both high volume and high-value sales (Average Price)
-  The Risk: Our revenue is highly concentrated in a few individuals, creating a dependency risk. 
- Strategic Recommendation: Implement a "Success Replication" program. Representatives like Monique and Harry, who show lower efficiency, require targeted training in closing strategies to increase their average ticket size and balance the team's contribution.

### 3. Historical Trends & Technical Diagnostic (Timeline)
- The Historical Sales Trend (2016-2019) shows consistent growth from monthly averages of $300 in 2016 to over $1,000 in peak months of 2019
- The 2019 "Drop" Explained: A surface-level look at the end of the timeline suggests a sharp decline in 2019. However, a professional data audit reveals a data cut-off bias.
- The Facts: Data for 2017 and 2018 represent full years, whereas 2019 records only reach July. We are comparing 12 months against roughly 58% of a year
- Strategic Recommendation: Historically, our highest peaks occur in the second half of the year. Management should prepare for a high-volume Q4, as 2019 is projected to maintain the upward growth trend seen since 2016 once the full year is accounted for.

## 🚀 Strategic Roadmap
- Diversify Performance: Reduce dependency risk by coaching underperforming representatives to replicate Pete’s high-value sales patterns.
- Digital Transformation: While not visible in the primary charts, internal analysis suggests our Website channel is a laggard; optimizing this platform could scale revenue significantly.
- Stabilize Growth: Implement seasonal stabilization strategies to manage the volatility observed in the monthly trends.
